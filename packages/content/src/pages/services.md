I work as a senior full-stack engineer and technical lead, usually embedded in a team that needs to move faster without breaking what already works. The framework matters less than the judgment around it, and judgment is where I add the most.

Most problems I am handed are not really about the code in front of me. They are about how the parts fit: the data model, the boundaries between services, the path a request actually takes. I map that first, then build the simplest thing that meets the requirement. Systems thinking like this is what produces architecture that survives changing requirements instead of buckling under them.

Quality only holds if the whole team can meet the same bar. I set up the static analysis, tests, and real coverage that make correct, legible code the default, I review with care, and I mentor as I go. At several places I was asked to lead where I had not been hired to, usually because the work around me had become more reliable.

The expensive mistakes are made at the whiteboard, not the keyboard. A premature abstraction, the wrong storage engine, a migration sequenced badly: each one costs months once it ships. I am the person in the room who asks the awkward question before the commitment is locked in, and who would rather prove the cheaper path with a small spike than argue it on a slide.

Not all technical debt is worth paying down. I help teams tell the difference, rank what is genuinely slowing delivery, and fold the repayment into normal feature work so the codebase gets healthier without a stop-the-world rewrite.

In practice that spans frontend architecture and design systems, performance auditing and optimisation, full-stack TypeScript development across React, Vue, and Angular, and framework migration and modernisation. If that sounds like the gap on your team, get in touch.
